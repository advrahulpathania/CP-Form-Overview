IBBI CP Forms

Simplified CIRP Monitoring Framework

From 9 Old Forms → 5 Streamlined Forms | Effective June 1, 2025

i

Why the Change Was Needed

  • Old system had 9 forms (IP-1 + CIRP 1–8)
  • Significant redundancy and overlapping data requirements
  • Multiple unpredictable event-driven deadlines
  • High administrative burden on Insolvency Professionals (IPs)
The new CP Forms reduce redundancy, introduce predictable monthly reporting, and enable auto-population of data on IBBI's digital platform for better efficiency.
i

New CP Forms: Purpose and Mapping

The revised framework consolidates information into stage-specific and monthly updates, ensuring timely and standardized data for effective IBBI oversight while easing compliance for IPs.

New CP Form CIRP Phase Covered Purpose – Why We Need to File This Form Replaces Old Forms
CP-1 Commencement → CoC Constitution To provide early-stage details (public announcement, claims received, CoC formation) for IBBI to monitor timely progress and creditor participation from the start. IP-1, CIRP-1, CIRP-2
CP-2 CoC Constitution → RFRP Issuance To report preparation of Information Memorandum, EoI received, and valuer appointments – enabling oversight on resolution applicant shortlisting. CIRP-3, CIRP-4
CP-3A Filing of Resolution/Liquidation Plan with AA To inform IBBI immediately when a resolution plan or liquidation application is submitted to NCLT for approval tracking. Part of CIRP-5
CP-3B AA Order on Plan/Liquidation To update on NCLT's approval/rejection of plan or initiation of liquidation – critical for final outcome monitoring. Part of CIRP-6
CP-4 Avoidance Transactions To report preferential, undervalued, fraudulent, or extortionate transactions identified – ensuring recovery efforts for creditor benefit are tracked. Part of CIRP-7
CP-5 Monthly Status Update To provide ongoing updates on CoC meetings, litigations, expenses, delays, and overall progress – allowing real-time IBBI monitoring of every active CIRP. New recurring requirement
Over 44% reduction in forms → Major relief for IPs with better data quality for regulators!

Filing Deadlines – Predictable and Simplified

Form Subject Due Date
CP-5 Monthly Status Update 10th of every month (for the previous month)
CP-1, CP-2, CP-3A, CP-4 Stage-specific events 10th of the subsequent month after the triggering event
CP-3B AA Order/Outcome Within 7 days of knowledge of NCLT order
Key recurring reminder: Submit CP-5 by the 10th of every month (Note: No penalties for delays in July–September 2025 transition period)
i

Penalties for Non-Compliance

  • ₹500 per form for each calendar month of delay (or part thereof)
  • Example: 3 months delay = ₹1,500 per form
  • Repeated delays may lead to warnings, disciplinary proceedings, or impact on renewal of Authorization for Assignment (AFA)

Transition Grace: No penalties for delayed filings during July–September 2025.

Now, Let's See How to File the CP Forms

All filings are mandatory on IBBI's dedicated electronic platform (accessible via www.ibbi.gov.in) using your unique credentials, DSC, or e-Sign.

1

Log in to the IBBI Electronic Platform using your credentials

2

Select the relevant Corporate Debtor / CIRP case

3

Choose the appropriate CP Form; benefit from auto-populated fields

4

Enter required data, attach supporting documents, and review thoroughly

5

Submit the form using DSC or e-Sign

Pro Tips:
• For any platform issues, contact support.form@ibbi.gov.in.
• Always ensure accuracy – IPs are responsible for complete and correct submissions.

Overview of IBBI CP Forms

This page provides a simplified overview of the new CP Forms framework introduced by IBBI effective June 1, 2025. It consolidates 9 old forms into 5 streamlined ones, introduces monthly reporting via CP-5, and aims to reduce compliance burden on Insolvency Professionals while improving regulatory oversight.

Key change: Shift from event-driven to predictable monthly deadlines with digital auto-population.

×

Detailed Explanation: Why the Change Was Needed

The previous system required filing 9 separate forms at unpredictable times tied to specific events, leading to redundancy (same data entered multiple times) and high administrative load. The new system eliminates overlaps, standardizes reporting, and uses technology for efficiency.

×

Detailed Explanation: New CP Forms

Each form covers specific phases and requires targeted information to enable stage-wise monitoring. Refer to the official circular for exact data fields and templates.

CP-5 is the cornerstone – a comprehensive monthly snapshot of the entire CIRP.

×

Detailed Explanation: Filing Deadlines

Most forms are due by the 10th of the next month, making compliance predictable. CP-3B is faster due to its importance in tracking outcomes.

During transition (July-Sep 2025), no penalties apply.

×

Detailed Explanation: Penalties

Regulation 40B imposes ₹500 per month per delayed form. Cumulative delays increase costs quickly. Consistent non-compliance can trigger regulatory action.

Grace period: No fees for July-September 2025 delays.

Detailed Explanation: Key Benefits

IPs save time; IBBI gets better data for supervision; overall CIRP becomes more transparent and efficient.
